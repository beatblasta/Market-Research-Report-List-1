<p><p>A microkeratome is a mechanical device used in ophthalmology to create a thin circular flap in the cornea of the eye during LASIK (Laser-Assisted In Situ Keratomileusis) surgery. It consists of a high-speed oscillating blade that is guided by a vacuum ring to create the corneal flap.</p><p>The microkeratome market is witnessing steady growth due to the increasing prevalence of vision disorders and the growing demand for corrective eye surgeries. The market is also driven by technological advancements in microkeratome devices, such as the integration of high-speed motors and automation features for enhanced precision and safety during surgery.</p><p>Moreover, the rising adoption of LASIK surgery due to its quick recovery time and high success rates is boosting the demand for microkeratomes. Additionally, the growing geriatric population, which is more prone to age-related vision problems, is contributing to the market growth.</p><p>The market is further propelled by increasing investments in healthcare infrastructure and the expansion of eye care facilities across various regions. Furthermore, favorable reimbursement policies for eye surgeries in several countries are creating a favorable market environment for microkeratome manufacturers.</p><p>In terms of trends, the market is witnessing a shift towards femtosecond lasers for creating corneal flaps instead of microkeratomes. Femtosecond lasers offer more precision and control during the surgery, reducing the risk of complications and improving outcomes. However, the high cost of femtosecond lasers remains a challenge for widespread adoption.</p><p>Overall, the microkeratome market is expected to grow at a healthy CAGR of 7.2% during the forecast period. Factors such as increasing vision disorders, technological advancements, and favorable healthcare policies are driving the market growth, while the trend towards femtosecond lasers presents both opportunities and challenges for microkeratome manufacturers.</p></p>

<p><p>The microkeratome is a medical device used in eye surgery to create a thin corneal flap. It is available in two market types: reusable and disposable. Reusable microkeratomes are made of durable materials and can be sterilized and reused multiple times, offering cost-efficiency in the long term.
