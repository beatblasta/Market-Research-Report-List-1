<p><p>Non-dairy whipping cream, also known as non-dairy whipped topping, is a plant-based alternative to traditional dairy-based whipping cream. Non-dairy whipping cream serves as a versatile ingredient for various desserts, pastries, and beverages, providing a creamy and rich texture.</p><p>The non-dairy whipping cream market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years, driven by the increasing demand for vegan and lactose-free products. Manufacturers are continuously developing new plant-based ingredients and improving the taste and texture of non-dairy whipping cream to meet the evolving consumer preferences.</p><p>Another trend is the increasing popularity of non-dairy whipping cream among the foodservice industry. Restaurants, cafes, and bakeries are incorporating non-dairy whipped cream into their menus to cater to the growing demand for vegan and lactose-free options. <p><p>The non-dairy whipping cream market comprises two main types: full-fat cream and low-fat cream. Full-fat cream is made by extracting fat content from plant sources, often coconut or palm kernel oil. It offers a rich and creamy texture, making it suitable for desserts and pastries. On the other hand, low-fat cream contains a reduced amount of fat, providing a lighter option for those seeking to reduce calorie intake or follow a healthier diet. Both variants cater to consumers' different preferences and dietary requirements within the non-dairy segment.</p></p>
