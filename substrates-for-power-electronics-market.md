<p><strong>The Substrates for Power Electronics Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Direct Bonded Copper (DBC) Substrates</li><li>AMB (Active Metal Brazed) Substrates</li><li>Insulated Metal Substrate (IMS)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Direct Bonded Copper (DBC) substrates are used in power electronics for their excellent thermal conductivity and electrical performance. These substrates are made by bonding copper to ceramic materials such as aluminum oxide or aluminum nitride. AMB (Active Metal Brazed) substrates feature a ceramic layer coated with a metal layer, which is joined through brazing. Insulated Metal Substrates (IMS) consist of a metal core with an insulating layer, offering superior thermal management. Other substrate types include ceramic, resin-based, and metal matrix composite substrates, each offering specific advantages in terms of electrical, thermal, and mechanical properties.</p></p>
