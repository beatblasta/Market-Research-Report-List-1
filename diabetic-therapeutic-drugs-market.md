<p><p>The diabetic therapeutic drugs market is divided into two main types: insulin therapies and non-insulin therapies. Insulin therapies involve the use of insulin to regulate blood sugar levels in individuals with diabetes. This can be administered through injections or insulin pumps. Non-insulin therapies, on the other hand, do not involve the use of insulin and include oral medications, injectable medications, and other alternative treatments. These therapies aim to control blood sugar levels by different mechanisms, such as enhancing insulin production or improving insulin utilization in the body.</p></p>
