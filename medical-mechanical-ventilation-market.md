<p><p>Medical mechanical ventilation, also known as positive pressure ventilation, is a lifesaving intervention used in critical care settings to ensure sufficient oxygen supply and remove carbon dioxide from the lungs of patients who are unable to breathe on their own or have inadequate breathing capabilities. It involves the use of mechanical ventilators, which deliver controlled volumes of air into the lungs through an endotracheal tube or a tracheostomy.</p><p>The medical mechanical ventilation market is witnessing significant growth due to the rising prevalence of respiratory diseases, such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), asthma, and acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS). Additionally, the growing geriatric population, increasing number of surgical procedures, and advancements in technology are driving market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has further accelerated the demand for medical mechanical ventilation devices as these are crucial in managing severe respiratory complications associated with the virus. The market has witnessed an unprecedented surge in demand during the pandemic.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth. These include the development of advanced features in mechanical ventilators, such as smart algorithms and integration of artificial intelligence, to enhance patient care and reduce ventilator-associated complications. There is also a shift towards portable and lightweight ventilators, allowing greater mobility and ease of use for patients.</p><p>According to the forecast, the medical mechanical ventilation market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 9.7% during the forecast period.
