<p><strong>The Intravenous (IV) Solutions Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Peripheral Parenteral Nutrition</li><li>Total Parenteral Nutrition</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Peripheral parenteral nutrition refers to the administration of nutrients via the veins in the arms, hands, or legs, providing a temporary source of nutrition when the digestive system cannot be used. Total parenteral nutrition, on the other hand, involves the delivery of a complete and balanced mixture of nutrients directly into the bloodstream through a central vein, bypassing the digestive system entirely. Both types of intravenous solutions are used when oral or enteral nutrition is not feasible or sufficient to meet a patient's nutritional needs.</p></p>
