<p><p>Offshore wind turbines come in various types including mono pile, jacket, tripod, and floating market. Mono pile turbines are the most common, consisting of a single steel pile anchored to the seabed. Jacket turbines have a lattice structure supporting the turbine, offering stability in deeper waters. Tripod turbines have three steel legs for support in challenging marine conditions. Floating market turbines are mobile and can be placed in deeper waters where fixed structures are not feasible. Each type caters to specific environmental and operational requirements in offshore wind energy production.</p></p>
