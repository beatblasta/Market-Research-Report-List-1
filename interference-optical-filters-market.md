<p><p>Interference optical filters are used in various applications to selectively transmit or block light of specific wavelengths. The High-Pass Type market offers filters that transmit light with wavelengths above a specified cutoff point, while the Low-Pass Type market consists of filters transmitting light with wavelengths below a specific cutoff. The Band-Pass Type market provides filters that selectively transmit a range of wavelengths within a certain bandwidth, while blocking others. These different filter types cater to diverse requirements in industries like telecommunications, photography, and spectroscopy.</p></p>
