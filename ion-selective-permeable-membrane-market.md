<p><p>Ion selective permeable membranes are a type of technology used in various applications such as water treatment, fuel cells, and sensors. Two common types of ion exchange membranes are perfluorosulfonic acid and perfluorocarboxylic acid membranes. Perfluorosulfonic acid membranes are known for their high proton conductivity and chemical stability, making them ideal for fuel cells. Perfluorocarboxylic acid membranes, on the other hand, offer excellent selectivity for specific ions, making them suitable for applications such as desalination and water purification.</p></p>
