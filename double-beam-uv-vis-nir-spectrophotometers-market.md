<p><p>Single Monochromator spectrophotometers use a single monochromator to disperse light into its different wavelengths, allowing for analysis within a specific range. They are simpler and more cost-efficient than their double monochromator counterparts. In contrast, Double Monochromator spectrophotometers use two monochromators to enhance the instrument's spectral resolution, enabling it to analyze a wider range of wavelengths with higher precision. Although more complex and expensive, double monochromator spectrophotometers are favored for demanding applications where accurate measurements across a broad wavelength range are required.</p></p>
