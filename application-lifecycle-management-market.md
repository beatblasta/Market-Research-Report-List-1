<p><p>Application Lifecycle Management (ALM) refers to the management of a software application from inception to retirement. The ALM market can be classified into two main types: On-Premise and Hosted. </p><p>On-Premise ALM involves hosting the ALM software on the client's own servers, giving them full control over its management, maintenance, and security. This type offers high customization but requires significant upfront investment and dedicated IT resources. </p><p>On the other hand, Hosted ALM, also known as Software as a Service (SaaS), involves accessing the ALM software through a provider's servers via the internet. It offers easy scalability, minimal infrastructure requirements, and regular updates, but customization options may be limited.</p></p>
