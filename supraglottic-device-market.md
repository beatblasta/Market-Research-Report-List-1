<p><p>A supraglottic device, also known as a supraglottic airway device, is a medical device used to maintain an open airway during procedures that require general anesthesia or for patients who have difficulty maintaining their own airway. These devices are placed in the oropharynx area just above the glottis and do not require intubation.</p><p>The supraglottic device market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. The market growth can be attributed to several factors such as the rising number of surgical procedures, increasing demand for emergency airway management devices, and the advantages of supraglottic devices over endotracheal tubes, such as ease of insertion and lower risk of trauma to the airway.</p><p>In addition, technological advancements in supraglottic devices have significantly improved their design and functionality, leading to increased adoption by healthcare professionals. The introduction of disposable supraglottic devices has also made these devices more convenient, hygienic, and cost-effective.</p><p>Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has further boosted the demand for supraglottic devices, especially for use in emergency and critical care settings. <p><p>The supraglottic device market comprises various types of devices used to facilitate airway management during medical procedures. Laryngeal mask airways are a commonly used type which are inserted into the lower pharynx to maintain a clear airway. Oropharyngeal airways are placed in the mouth to keep the tongue away from the back of the throat, allowing better air flow. Nasopharyngeal airways are inserted through the nose to secure the airway. Other types of devices include esophageal-tracheal Combitubes and pharyngeal tracheal lumen airways, which also help ensure proper airflow and oxygenation during medical interventions.</p></p>
