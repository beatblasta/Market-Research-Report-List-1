<p><p>A filtered fume hood is a type of laboratory containment and ventilation system that is designed to remove hazardous chemicals, fumes, and gases from the air, thereby ensuring a safe working environment for laboratory personnel. Unlike traditional fume hoods, which rely on external exhaust systems, filtered fume hoods utilize filtration technology to remove contaminants before recirculating clean air back into the laboratory.</p><p>The filtered fume hood market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing emphasis on workplace safety and the rising awareness about the harmful effects of chemical exposure. The global market for filtered fume hoods is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.9% during the forecast period. </p><p>One of the key trends driving market growth is the growing adoption of energy-efficient filtered fume hoods. These hoods are designed to reduce energy consumption by employing advanced airflow control systems and low-pressure-drop filters. Energy-efficient filtered fume hoods not only provide substantial cost savings to laboratories but also contribute to sustainability efforts by reducing carbon footprints.</p><p>Another trend reshaping the filtered fume hood market is the integration of smart technologies.
