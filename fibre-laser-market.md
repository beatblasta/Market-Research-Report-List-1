<p><p>Fibre laser is a type of laser that uses optical fibers as the active medium to generate and amplify light. The fiber laser combines the advantages of solid-state lasers and semiconductor lasers, resulting in a compact, efficient, and high-performance laser technology. It offers several advantages such as high beam quality, high efficiency, narrow linewidth, and reliability. These characteristics make it suitable for various applications including materials processing, automotive, aerospace, medical, and telecommunications, among others.</p><p>The fibe laser market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 5.5% during the forecast period. <p><ul><li>Continuous Wave (CW) Fibre Laser</li><li>Pulsed Fibre Laser</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fibre laser market consists of two main types: continuous wave (CW) fibre lasers and pulsed fibre lasers. Continuous wave fibre lasers emit a continuous beam of laser light, providing a steady energy output. They are commonly used in applications such as micromachining and material processing. Pulsed fibre lasers, on the other hand, emit short pulses of laser light, resulting in high peak powers. They are often used in fields like telecommunications and precision surgery where precise timing is crucial.</p></p>
