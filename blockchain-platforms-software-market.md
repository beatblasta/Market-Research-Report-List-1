<p><p>Blockchain platforms software market can be categorized into three types: private, public, and consortium. </p><p>Private blockchain platforms are restricted to specific organizations and require permission to participate. These platforms provide enhanced privacy and control to the organizations involved but limit accessibility to external parties. </p><p>Public blockchain platforms, on the other hand, are open to anyone and allow for transparent and decentralized transactions. Public platforms offer greater security due to their distributed nature but may lack privacy controls.</p><p>Consortium blockchain platforms are a hybrid of both, forming a network where multiple organizations collaborate to achieve shared goals while maintaining a controlled participation model. They offer a balance of security, control, and accessibility between private and public platforms.</p></p>
