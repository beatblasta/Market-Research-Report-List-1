<p><p>Peanut flour is a powdered form of peanuts that is produced by grinding roasted and defatted peanuts. It is a high-protein and gluten-free product often used as a nutritional supplement, flavoring agent, and a substitute for wheat flour in various food applications. Peanut flour is rich in nutrients such as vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making it a popular choice among health-conscious consumers.</p><p>The peanut flour market has been experiencing significant growth due to several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for gluten-free products, driven by the rising prevalence of celiac disease and gluten intolerance, has led to a higher adoption of peanut flour as a substitute for traditional wheat flour. Additionally, the growing popularity of plant-based diets and the rising vegan and vegetarian population have further fueled the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the shift towards healthier snacking options and the increasing use of peanut flour in the food processing industry as a flavor enhancer and binder have also contributed to the market expansion. Peanut flour is widely used in the production of baked goods, confectioneries, beverages, and savory snacks, among others.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing various trends that are likely to drive its growth in the coming years. <p><p>The peanut flour market is categorized into three types: light, medium, and dark. Light peanut flour has a mild flavor and is made from peanuts with their skins removed. Medium peanut flour has a slightly stronger taste as it is made from peanuts with some of their skins intact. Dark peanut flour has the strongest flavor as it is made from peanuts with a higher percentage of skins. These different types cater to varying preferences and are used in a wide range of culinary applications.</p></p>
