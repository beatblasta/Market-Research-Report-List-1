<p><p>Micro-Invasive Glaucoma Surgery (MIGS) devices are a minimally invasive treatment option for glaucoma. The market consists of two types: implants and blink-assisted devices. Implants are small devices placed in the eye to improve the drainage of fluid, reducing intraocular pressure. Blink-assisted devices, on the other hand, are specifically designed to enhance the body's natural blinking mechanism, thereby enhancing fluid drainage. Both types of devices aim to provide a safer and less invasive alternative to traditional glaucoma surgeries.</p></p>
