<p><ul><li>Non-stick Pad</li><li>Sticky Pad</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Wound dressing pads are available in two main types: non-stick pads and sticky pads. Non-stick pads are designed to be gentle on wounds and prevent the dressing from sticking to the wound surface, ensuring painless removal. They are commonly used for minor cuts and burns. On the other hand, sticky pads have adhesive properties to securely attach the dressing to the wound, providing better protection and preventing any shifting. These are generally used for larger wounds or areas that require more secure and long-lasting coverage.</p></p>

<p><p>Wound dressing pads are widely used in the healthcare industry for the treatment and management of various types of wounds. They find application in three major categories: chronic wounds, acute wounds, and postoperative wounds. Chronic wounds refer to long-term injuries that are slow to heal, such as ulcers or pressure sores. Acute wounds are sudden injuries, like cuts or burns, that require immediate attention. Postoperative wounds, as the name suggests, are surgical wounds that need dressing after an operation to promote healing and reduce infections. Wound dressing pads are designed to provide protection, absorb fluids, and create a favorable healing environment for these different types of wounds.</p></p>
