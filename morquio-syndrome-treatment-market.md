<p><p>Morquio syndrome, also known as mucopolysaccharidosis type IV (MPS IV), is a rare genetic disorder that affects the body's ability to break down long chains of sugar molecules called glycosaminoglycans (GAGs). The accumulation of GAGs leads to several health issues such as skeletal abnormalities, organ damage, and reduced mobility. The treatment of Morquio syndrome primarily focuses on managing the symptoms and improving the patient's quality of life.</p><p>Enzyme replacement therapy (ERT) is one of the main treatment options for Morquio syndrome. ERT involves providing the body with the missing or deficient enzyme responsible for breaking down GAGs. This therapy helps slow down disease progression and improve certain symptoms. Another approach is hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT), which can potentially slow down the progression of skeletal abnormalities.</p><p>The market for Morquio syndrome treatment is expected to grow at a significant rate during the forecast period.
