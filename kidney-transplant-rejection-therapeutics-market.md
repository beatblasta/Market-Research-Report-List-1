<p><p>The kidney transplant rejection therapeutics market consists of various types of medications used to prevent rejection after a kidney transplant. Azathioprine is an immunosuppressant that hinders the immune system's response. Cortina is a corticosteroid that reduces inflammation and suppresses the immune system. Cyclophosphamide is another immunosuppressant that specifically targets rapidly dividing cells. Cyclosporin A is a drug that suppresses the immune system and is commonly used in kidney transplant patients. Other therapeutics in this market include medications such as mycophenolate mofetil and basiliximab, which also aim to prevent rejection by suppressing the immune system.</p></p>
