<p><p>The surgical anti-adhesion market refers to the market for products that prevent adhesion formation following surgery. This can include various types such as gels, sheets, and drugs. Gels are substances applied to the surgical site to create a physical barrier between tissues, preventing them from sticking together. Sheets are thin materials that can be placed between tissues to act as a barrier. Drugs, on the other hand, are medications that can be administered systemically or locally to prevent adhesion formation. These different types offer surgeons various options for preventing adhesions in surgical procedures.</p></p>
