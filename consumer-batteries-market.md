<p><p>Consumer batteries can be classified into several types. Nickel Cadmium (NiCad) batteries are known for their high power output and long cycle life, making them suitable for power tools and toys. Nickel-Metal Hydride batteries are commonly used in portable electronics due to their higher energy density and longer run times. Alkaline batteries are popular due to their affordability and versatility, powering everyday household devices. Primary Lithium batteries offer long shelf life and superior performance, often used in medical devices and cameras. Li-Ion batteries, known for their high energy density and rechargeability, are commonly found in smartphones and laptops.</p></p>
