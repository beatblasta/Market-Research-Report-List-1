<p><p>Protein engineering involves modifying and creating proteins for various purposes. On the other hand, irrational protein design involves randomly modifying proteins without prior knowledge, often through techniques like directed evolution, to generate new functionalities. Both rational and irrational protein design markets cater to the demand for custom-designed proteins with tailor-made properties and applications in fields like medicine, agriculture, and industry.</p></p>
