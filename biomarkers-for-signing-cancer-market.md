<p><p>Biomarkers for cancer can be classified into three main types: genetic biomarkers, protein biomarkers, and glyco-biomarkers. Genetic biomarkers are specific DNA alterations that help identify certain cancer types and predict treatment responses. Protein biomarkers are specific proteins present in the body that can indicate the presence or progression of cancer. Glyco-biomarkers are certain sugar molecules attached to proteins or lipids that can also offer valuable information about cancer diagnosis and prognosis. These three types of biomarkers play crucial roles in the detection, monitoring, and treatment of cancer.</p></p>
