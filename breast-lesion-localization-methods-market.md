<p><p>Breast lesion localization methods are medical techniques used to accurately locate and mark abnormal areas in the breast before surgical removal. These methods help guide surgeons during procedures such as lumpectomy or biopsy, ensuring that the target lesion is effectively localized and removed with minimal damage to surrounding healthy tissue.</p><p>Various breast lesion localization methods are available, including wire localization, radioisotope localization, magnetic seed localization, and reflector-based localization. Wire localization involves the insertion of a thin wire into the breast to mark the lesion's location. Radioisotope localization uses a radioactive seed or injection to pinpoint the abnormal area. Magnetic seed localization involves the placement of a small magnetic seed within the breast tissue. Reflector-based localization uses a combination of ultrasound and a unique marker reflector to locate and guide the surgeon to the target lesion.</p><p>The breast lesion localization methods market is rapidly growing due to increasing awareness about the importance of early breast cancer detection. Rising prevalence of breast cancer, technological advancements in localization methods, and a shift towards minimally invasive surgeries are driving market growth.
