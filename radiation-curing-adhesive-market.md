<p><p>Radiation curing adhesives are a type of adhesive that cure quickly when exposed to specific types of radiation. The two main types of radiation curing adhesives are Ultraviolet (UV) curing and Electron Beam (EB) curing. UV curing adhesives use UV light to initiate a curing process, while EB curing adhesives use high-energy electrons to cure the adhesive. Both types offer fast curing times, strong bonds, and environmentally friendly characteristics, making them popular choices in industries such as electronics, automotive, and packaging.</p></p>
