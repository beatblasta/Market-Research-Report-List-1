<p><ul><li>Physical Hydrogel</li><li>Chemical Hydrogel</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Synthetic macromolecule hydrogels are classified into two main types: physical hydrogels and chemical hydrogels. Physical hydrogels are formed through physical interactions such as hydrogen bonding or van der Waals forces, while chemical hydrogels are created through covalent bonds or cross-linking reactions. Physical hydrogels have reversible properties and respond to external stimuli, making them suitable for drug delivery and tissue engineering applications. Chemical hydrogels offer stronger and more stable networks, making them ideal for long-term implantation in biomedical devices and controlled release systems.</p></p>
