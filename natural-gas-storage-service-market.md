<p><p>The natural gas storage service market consists of three main types: underground, above ground, and floating natural gas storage. Underground storage involves storing natural gas in depleted oil and gas reservoirs, aquifers, or salt caverns, allowing for large volumes to be held for longer periods. Above ground storage includes using containers or tanks to store natural gas, providing more flexibility in terms of location and capacity. Floating storage, on the other hand, involves storing liquefied natural gas (LNG) on special ships or barges, allowing for transportation and storage in areas without infrastructure.</p></p>
