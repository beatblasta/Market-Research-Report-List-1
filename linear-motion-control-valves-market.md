<p><p>Linear motion control valves are used to regulate the flow of fluids or gases in a linear manner. Gate valves are operated through the sliding motion of a gate mechanism, providing effective shut-off capabilities. Ball valves have a rotating ball with a hole that controls the flow, offering quick shut-off and precise flow control. Diaphragm valves use a flexible diaphragm that moves up and down to control the flow. Other types include globe valves, needle valves, and pinch valves, which function based on varying mechanisms but serve the same purpose of fluid regulation.</p></p>
